It all began when I discovered the [latest release](https://nftnow.com/guides/midjourney-5-2-a-guide-to-the-latest-tools-and-features/) of Midjourney. Curiosity sparked within me, prompting me to put the Zoom-out features to the test and share with my [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/p/CuFqHAnxarS/) friends.

Join me as I share the steps I took and the prompts I used to create a captivating video that showcases the artistic exploration I embarked upon.

**Setting the Stage with a Midjourney Prompt**
----------------------------------------------

To begin my artistic journey, I selected the following prompt: “An illustration of a cute girl, super closeup on the face, in the style of Pendleton Ward from Adventure Time.”

This prompt allowed me to explore different styles within Midjourney, beyond the typical photography style.

![](https://storage.googleapis.com/papyrus_images/5061163eb7aa8c83d479d94eaacf0a78cf897e31ef7d8d760142aeabbfc5eab4.png)

The “closeup” type of image is extremely important to give us room to create **different surroundings** but with a fixed character. Tweak your prompt until you get a good close-up.

**Introducing the Glass Orb Concept**
-------------------------------------

During my exploration, I noticed a slight warping effect caused by Midjourney as I kept zooming out, see image below. To address this, I devised a simple solution: placing the entire scene **within a glass orb**.

![I must confess that the autumn orb image is my favorite from all this experiment.](https://storage.googleapis.com/papyrus_images/a11e011feb0d0889105d5fa383f90082526b920a49ff6af47104ea12b9b0e76a.png)

I must confess that the autumn orb image is my favorite from all this experiment.

*Prompt: “In a glass ball --ar 3:2 --zoom 2.”*

This approach not only mitigated the warping effect, but also sparked the idea of multiverses. The glass orb became a gateway to endless artistic possibilities.

**Expanding the Multiverse with Glass Balls in the Forest**

**Post-Processing and Editing in CapCut**
-----------------------------------------

After completing the prompts in Midjourney, I turned to [CapCut](https://www.capcut.com/) for the post-processing and editing phase. Capcut is a powerful editing tool that allowed me to enhance and refine the visual elements of my video. Here are some of the features I utilized:

1. **Alignment**: I aligned the individual images generated by Midjourney to create a seamless visual flow. This ensured that each frame transitioned smoothly into the next, maintaining the coherence of the video.
2. **Synchronization to Music**: To enhance the emotional impact of the video, I synchronized the images to a chosen song, with “epic” theme. I timed specific transitions and visual cues to align with the beats and rhythm of the music, improving the viewing experience.
3. **Zoom 1 Entry Animation**: Between the zoom-out scenes, I employed Capcut's "zoom 1" entry animation to add a subtle and visually appealing effect. For most images I set the movement to last almost as long as that frame.
4. **Clean Cuts**: During the sections where the chosen character (the girl, the cat, or the frame) traveled through different seasons, I opted for clean cuts. Clean cuts involve directly transitioning between frames without any additional animation. This technique created a crisp and impactful change between the different landscapes.
5. **Varied Frame Durations**: To maintain viewer engagement and avoid monotony, I carefully adjusted the frame durations throughout the video. By varying the length of each frame, I ensured that the pacing remained dynamic and engaging, capturing the viewers' attention.

![Zoom entry animation](https://storage.googleapis.com/papyrus_images/603207de6a80f901bcffa6c1c44f22228bec30c49c694b6331b16e2ec3e17d50.png)

The combination of these CapCut features allowed me to craft a visually stunning and emotionally captivating video.

The editing process was a meticulous endeavor, ensuring that every frame and transition harmonized with the overarching narrative I had in mind.
